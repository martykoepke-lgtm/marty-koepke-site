# The AI Visibility Self-Assessment

## Score your business in 5 minutes

A six-dimension scorecard for established small businesses.

By Practical Informatics — practicalinformatics.com

---

## Why this matters

Something quietly changed in the last 18 months: a meaningful share of your buyers stopped Googling and started asking ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity for recommendations.

- **73% of B2B buyers now research vendors using AI agents.** *(Forrester, 2026)*
- **AI search traffic grew 42.8% year-over-year** while Google grew 2.4%. *(SimilarWeb, 2026)*
- Most of this happens **invisibly**. The customer asks AI, gets a recommendation (or doesn't), and you never see the visit in your analytics.

When your buyer asks an AI agent for a recommendation in your category, you are either named — or you're not. If you're not, you've lost a lead you never knew existed.

This scorecard takes about five minutes. It tells you where you stand across the six dimensions that determine whether AI knows, recommends, and accurately describes your business.

---

## How to use this scorecard

- **30 yes / no questions** — five per dimension
- **1 point per yes**, **5 points possible per dimension**, **30 total**
- Be honest — there's no prize for a high score, only useful information
- Count your yeses as you go in the score box at the end of each dimension
- At the end, find your tier and decide what to do next

A note: most established small businesses score between **8 and 18** their first time through. If you score lower, that's not failure — it's a starting line.

# Dimension 3: Entity Clarity

*Can AI tell exactly who and where you are — without confusion?*

1. Are your business name, address, and phone (NAP) consistent across your website, Google Business Profile, and LinkedIn?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
2. Is your business name unique enough that AI won't confuse you with similarly-named entities?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
3. Can a stranger find a clear one-sentence description of what you do, on your home page, in under 3 seconds?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
4. Is your founder's identity clear (name, role, pronouns where used) without scrolling?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
5. Does your contact info (phone, email, address) appear consistently in at least three places online?    ☐ Yes ☐ No

# Dimension 5: Structured Data

*Does your site speak the machine-readable language LLMs use?*

1. Does your site have an `llms.txt` file? (Check at `yourdomain.com/llms.txt`)    ☐ Yes ☐ No
2. Does your site have Organization JSON-LD schema? (View page source → search `application/ld+json`)    ☐ Yes ☐ No
3. Does your site have Person JSON-LD schema for the founder?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
4. Does your site have FAQPage JSON-LD schema on FAQ-type content?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
5. Does your site have OpenGraph and Twitter Card meta tags on every page?    ☐ Yes ☐ No

# Dimension 6: Agent and Citation Graph

*Does the wider web confirm what your site says about you?*

1. Does your `robots.txt` explicitly allow `GPTBot`, `ClaudeBot`, or `PerplexityBot`? (Check `yourdomain.com/robots.txt`)    ☐ Yes ☐ No
2. Are you mentioned by name on at least three external websites (directories, press, podcasts, guest posts) you don't own?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
3. Do you have a complete LinkedIn presence (personal profile + company page where relevant)?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
4. Are you listed in at least three industry directories or vertical-specific platforms?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
5. Do you have a Wikipedia or Wikidata entry — or at least a high-authority mention (named industry publication, well-known podcast, recognized conference)?    ☐ Yes ☐ No
