# Practical Informatics — Vision

**Author:** Marty Koepke, Founder
**Status:** Working document, v1.0
**Purpose:** The strategic north star. Read this when a decision is hard. Update when the world changes.

---

## In one paragraph

Practical Informatics is a small, focused consulting practice that helps established small businesses, solo founders, and small practices become visible to the AI agents their buyers now ask. We do that as a translator and a partner — not as a dashboard or a retainer — by measuring what AI is actually saying, explaining it in plain English, doing the remediation alongside the client, and proving the number moved. Built on twenty years of healthcare informatics — the discipline of translating between technology, leadership, and the people who use the tools — applied to the discovery layer that just moved out from under everyone.

---

## 1. The shift we're built on

Something quietly changed in the last 18 months. A meaningful share of buyers stopped Googling and started asking ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity for recommendations. By every credible measure, 70-plus percent of B2B buyers now research vendors using AI. Consumers do the same for restaurants, travel, services, almost everything. The discovery layer moved, and most established small businesses haven't noticed because the change is invisible inside Google Analytics. The buyer asks AI, gets a recommendation (or doesn't), and the business never sees the visit.

This is the most important sentence in the strategy: **when your buyer asks an AI agent for a recommendation in your category, you are either named — or you're not. If you're not, you've lost a lead you never knew existed.**

That single fact is what Practical Informatics exists to address.

---

## 2. Why now is the moment, specifically for small players

Two things make this a once-in-a-decade window:

**The fixes are mostly mechanical.** Schema, llms.txt, robots.txt agent permissions, structured About content, Google Business Profile, third-party corroboration — these are repeatable, teachable, and within reach for a small business owner working with the right partner.

**The math is structurally egalitarian.** The peer-reviewed research (Princeton's "GEO" paper, 2024) found that lower-ranked pages benefit most from AI-visibility work — up to 115% citation lift for content at position 5, versus near-zero for position-1 incumbents. The incumbents already won SEO. They didn't win this. AI search is the rare surface where a solo consultant, a foothills clinic, or a regional firm can earn citations the giants haven't locked up.

We are not selling people on a hard problem. We are selling them on a moment where the work pays disproportionately — if they move now.

Established small businesses where buyers research before they decide.

The buyer we serve has a specific shape: smart enough to know they need help, not a marketer, allergic to jargon, suspicious of hype, willing to pay for honest work done by a human they trust. That shape is who every decision in the business optimizes for.

## 4. What we are (the category)

**Done-with-you AI visibility partner.**

That phrase is doing a lot of work. Let me unpack each piece.

**"Done-with-you,"** not done-for-you and not do-it-yourself. The buyer we serve doesn't have the staff for done-for-you ($10K/mo agency retainers), and they don't have the skill or time for do-it-yourself (read fifty blog posts and become a schema expert). They need someone to do the hardest parts beside them, teach them what's transferable, and hand off what's maintainable.

**"AI visibility,"** not SEO and not generalist marketing. We have one specialty. The specialty is fresh enough that we can be the early credible authority in our niche. We don't bolt it onto something else.

**"Partner,"** not vendor. A vendor sells a thing and walks away. A partner shows up, stays through the work, proves the number moved, and is still there when the next thing happens. The relationship is the product.

We sit in the gap between SaaS tools (sell information to people who already know what to do with it) and traditional agencies (sell implementation in marketing jargon to companies with budget). We are the only ones who translate and implement for a buyer who is not a marketer, inside a niche where trust is the currency.

## 5. What we believe (the principles that govern everything)

These are how we work, what we refuse to do, and what we will never apologize for.

**Process before tools.** Most "AI problems" are process problems wearing a costume. We look at how things flow before we recommend any technology. Sometimes the answer is AI; sometimes it's a smarter process and no AI at all. We say which is which.

**AI where it belongs, not where it's trendy.** AI is a tool. We use it where it is genuinely the right answer, and we tell the client plainly when it isn't. The "AI" in our name describes a discovery layer, not a religion.

**Translation over dashboards.** The whole point is plain English. The client gets what is happening, why it matters, and what to do. They never need to become a marketer to understand the recommendation.

**Top five, not top fifty.** A 50-item fix list isn't actionable; it's overwhelming. We surface what we find, but the recommendations are the five highest-leverage moves. Do those. Then come back.

**Transparency over mystique.** We publish our prices. We publish our rubric. We publish our methodology. We publish the research we built it on. We tell clients when we're not sure about something. The hype sellers promise certainty; we sell honest measurement.

**Prove it moved.** Every paid engagement includes a baseline, the work, and a re-measure that shows the change. No "trust us, we did the thing." Show the number.

**Fair to the competition.** When we compare ourselves to SaaS tools or agencies, we describe what they're genuinely good at before we describe where they fall short. A comparison that strawmans the competition undercuts the trust-first brand. We compete by being more accurate, not louder.

**The buyer's time is sacred.** No 30-page reports. No 90-minute "discovery calls" that are really pitches. No nurture sequences that pretend to be content. If we waste their time, we don't deserve their money.

## 6. The product — what we sell and how it ladders

A four-tier ladder, designed so every tier is honest about what it is.

**Free — AI-Readiness Check.** Top of funnel. Answers *"are you built to be found?"* Takes thirty seconds. Email-gated for the full report. The job: prove the problem is real and that we know what we're doing.

**Paid — AI Visibility Index Report (~$1,000).** Answers *"are you actually being found, where are the gaps, and what do you do about it?"* Full live cross-engine measurement, seven-dimension scorecard, top-five prioritized fixes, 30-minute walk-through call. **The report fee credits 100% toward a Sprint if booked within 30 days** — so the report is an on-ramp, not a dead end.

**Done-with-you — Remediation Sprint ($3K–$5K).** Foundations or Expanded, depending on the size of the footprint (locations, services, competitors, platforms). 30–60 day engagement. The priority fixes done with the client, plus a 60-day re-measure that proves the number moved.

**Ongoing — Visibility Partner ($600/mo, optional, post-Sprint only).** Monthly monitoring, quarterly re-measures, continued guidance. Not a standalone retainer — only offered to clients who have completed a Sprint and want to keep growing.

The core revenue mechanic is the Report → Sprint conversion. The Report is where we earn the right to do the work; the Sprint is where the work happens; the Partner is where the work compounds.

## 7. The standard we hold the work to

Every deliverable answers four questions before it ships:

1. **Is the measurement valid?** Does it measure what it claims to measure?
2. **Is it reliable?** Same input, same score — across runs and across analysts.
3. **Is it calculable?** Does it produce a number, not an opinion?
4. **Is it honest?** Would I be comfortable defending every number to a skeptical client over coffee?

That last question is the one that kills most consulting work. We will not ship anything we cannot defend in plain English to a smart person who wants to argue with us.

The rubric is the artifact that makes this true. It is published, anchored, weighted, and grounded in cited research. Two analysts score the same business and converge. A client re-measures sixty days later and the lift is real. The methodology is the credibility engine. We do not hide it.

## 8. What we are deliberately not building

Saying no is what makes the strategy real.

**We are not building a SaaS dashboard.** The measurement market is already crowded — Profound at a $1B valuation, Otterly, Peec, Scrunch. They built better dashboards than we ever will. We are not funded for that war and don't want to fight it. Our advantage is interpretation, remediation, and trust — not the artifact.

**We are not building a generalist marketing agency.** We don't do ads, we don't do brand strategy, we don't do website builds from scratch, we don't do "let's audit your whole funnel." We do one thing well.

**We are not promising rankings or guarantees.** The discipline is contextual, non-static, and partly stochastic. We promise honest measurement and the highest-leverage fixes. We do not promise outcomes that depend on factors no one can control.

**We are not chasing every niche.** Healthcare-adjacent practices, professional services, regional firms, and personal brands with real domain expertise are the target. We can serve other small businesses well, but we don't market broadly. Niche-trust is the moat.

**We are not building a course business — yet.** The methodology is teachable. Teaching it is a real future path. But it happens after we have demonstrated results, not before. Teaching from theory while we sell the doing of the thing would erode the trust-first brand. Earn the evidence first.

## 9. The longer arc (where this could go in 18–36 months)

Today we are a one-person practice with a productized service. The credible next moves, in rough sequence:

**Year 1 — Establish.** Land the first 20–30 paid Reports. Convert ~half to Sprints. Build the case-study library. Publish at least one benchmark study (e.g., the ambient-AI clinical documentation field) as flagship content that produces inbound leads and demonstrates the method on businesses bigger than ours.

**Year 1–2 — Codify.** Calibrate the rubric weights from real engagements (the v0.1 weights are starting hypotheses). Refine query-template libraries per niche. Document the playbook so it can survive a hand-off. Begin a deliberate content cadence — LinkedIn carousels, YouTube transparent-process videos, a recurring "I tested it" segment that doubles as content and as visibility research.

**Year 2 — Multiply through teaching.** Once we have a defensible methodology and a portfolio of proven engagements, the rubric becomes licensable IP. The natural shape: a "certified AVI practitioner" program that trains other consultants to add AI visibility as a service line. Higher-ticket than business-owner DIY courses, no cannibalization, and it turns the methodology from a service into an asset.

**Year 2–3 — Expand the partnership tier.** Ongoing Visibility Partner becomes a more substantial part of revenue as the case-study library proves the recurring value. Some clients move from Sprint to Partner to longer-term advisory relationships. Capacity stays capped by founder hours — but the per-hour rate climbs as the brand compounds.

**The plateau we don't try to break.** We do not become an agency with employees. We do not raise funding. We do not chase scale that requires a sales team. The size of the business is bounded by what a single founder can deliver well, with selective contract help and educational leverage. That ceiling is high enough.

## 10. The voice

How we talk and write is part of the brand.

- **Plain English, always.** No jargon. No buzzwords. If a sentence needs a marketing dictionary to parse, it doesn't ship.
- **Sentence case headings.** Not Title Case Like A Press Release.
- **Calm, not urgent.** No countdown timers. No "only 3 spots left." We are the quiet authority, not the carnival barker.
- **Honest about uncertainty.** "The literature suggests…" beats "It is proven that…" when it should. "We don't know yet" is a complete sentence.
- **Specific, not vague.** "Adding original statistics improved citation visibility 41% in the Princeton study" beats "AI rewards original content."
- **Generous to the reader.** Footnote the source. Link to the rubric. Show the work. Treat the reader as a smart adult who can handle the truth.

## 11. The standard we hold ourselves to as a business

A few personal commitments that shape how this is run:

**No client we wouldn't recommend to a friend.** If we don't believe the engagement will move the number, we say no.

**No work outside the rails.** No PHI in any AI tool we touch. No clients in industries we don't understand. No favors that compromise the methodology.

**The founder's name is on every report.** Marty reviews every paid deliverable before it goes out. The automation produces it; she vouches for it.

**Margin protects the work.** Solo-founder boutiques die from scope creep more than from undercharging. Every engagement has defined deliverables, defined timeline, defined revision rounds, defined "what's not included." Boundaries are how a boutique stays profitable enough to deliver well.

**The brand is the long bet.** Every short-term tradeoff is judged against whether it earns or erodes trust with a buyer who hasn't decided yet whether to call us. When in doubt, optimize for trust.
