# AI is becoming a layer between your buyers and your business

When a buyer asks ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, or Gemini for a recommendation in your category, the answer may not look like a list of search results. It may look like a confident summary, a comparison, or a single business named first.

What that layer says about you is what your buyer believes — often before they ever visit your site.

Sometimes AI gets it right. Often, it doesn't.

## What is actually happening to your business right now

AI can fail a business in four distinct ways. Each looks different. Each costs something different.

**AI doesn't surface you at all.** Buyers ask the question your business is built to answer. AI names someone else and never mentions you. You don't lose to a better answer; you lose to a different recommendation that never had to consider you.

**AI surfaces you, but with wrong facts.** It places you in the wrong city. It says you offer services you don't. It uses an old phone number, an old address, an old version of who you serve. Buyers form a first impression on information that wasn't accurate when AI said it.

**AI surfaces you, but flattens what makes you different.** Your method, your specialization, your point of view — none of it survives the summary. AI describes you in the same generic language it uses for every competitor in your category. You become indistinguishable.

**AI cites your page but recommends a competitor.** Your content shows up in the answer's source list. Your name doesn't. You contributed the explanation; someone else got the buyer.

## What you need to know about how AI works

Traditional SEO was built for a system that crawled pages and ranked them. AI search does something different — and that difference is where the new failure modes live.

**AI forms answers; it doesn't return lists.** Many AI systems use retrieval-augmented generation to assemble an answer from outside sources. A page can still be technically "in the index" and not appear in the answer, because the AI didn't select your passage. ([Gao et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.10997))

**AI selects passages, not pages.** Surfer's research found that 67.82% of cited sources in Google AI Overviews do not rank in Google's top 10 for the same query. A well-ranked page can be invisible to AI if the actual passage isn't reusable. ([Surfer](https://surferseo.com/blog/llm-citations/))

**AI can lose your context when it breaks your content apart.** Anthropic's work on contextual retrieval makes this concrete: a chunk that says "the company's revenue grew by 3%" is meaningless if it doesn't carry which company, and when. The same is true for any claim that depended on its surroundings to be accurate. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/contextual-retrieval))

**AI can sound authoritative when it's wrong.** The Tow Center tested eight generative search tools across 1,600 queries and found incorrect answers more than 60% of the time, often delivered confidently. Premium chatbots sometimes gave more confidently wrong answers than free ones. ([Columbia Journalism Review](https://www.cjr.org/tow_center/we-compared-eight-ai-search-engines-theyre-all-bad-at-citing-news.php))

Each of these is a failure mode that traditional SEO measurement was not designed to catch.

## What is different about how we measure this

Most AI visibility tools answer a single question:

> Did AI mention us?

We answer a different one:

> Did AI get us right?

The framework has two layers, and they answer different questions.

**Readiness** asks whether your business is giving AI the right evidence to work with. It is the work on your site, your Google Business Profile, and the third-party sources AI actually uses. One example: *business clarity* — can AI tell who you are, what you do, and who you help, from the public footprint you've already built?

**Measured outcomes** ask what AI actually says when we test it with the questions your buyer asks. One example: *claim support* — when AI says something specific about you, can we find a real source that proves it?

Readiness is what you can change. Outcomes are how AI is behaving today. We measure both, separately, and never confuse the two.
