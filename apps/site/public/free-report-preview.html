Priority improvements

3 ways to improve your AI visibility

These are practical next moves based on the weakest readiness signals we could observe from your public site. They are not a substitute for live AI answer testing, but they are good places to start.

  1. 1

    Support the claims you want AI to repeat

    Do this: Add proof next to important claims: case examples, named credentials, source links, testimonials, screenshots, or short evidence notes.

    Why it helps: Unsupported claims are harder for AI to trust and cite. Evidence gives AI a reason to repeat the claim confidently.

    Example: If the site says “AI visibility audit,” add a short example showing what was measured, what changed, and which source or result supports it.

  2. 2

    Tell AI when you are the right recommendation

    Do this: Add explicit best-fit and not-fit language to service pages so AI knows which buyer situations should point toward you.

    Why it helps: AI recommendations improve when the source material includes conditions. Without fit signals, AI may recommend you too broadly or skip you for safer generic options.

    Example: “Best fit for founder-led service businesses with a real offer and public website. Not a fit for ecommerce brands looking for paid media management.”

  3. 3

    Give AI a specific reason to choose you

    Do this: Name your method, framework, or point of view, then explain what tradeoff it makes and why it matters to the buyer.

    Why it helps: When several businesses sound similar, AI defaults to generic recommendations. A distinct, supportable perspective helps it understand why you are different.

    Example: “Our approach measures accuracy before visibility, because being recommended for the wrong reason can be worse than not showing up.”
