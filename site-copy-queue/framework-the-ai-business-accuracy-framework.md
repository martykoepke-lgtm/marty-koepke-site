# The AI Business Accuracy Framework

AI visibility tells you whether AI mentions your business.

AI Business Accuracy tells you whether AI gets your business right.

The AI Business Accuracy Framework is a practical way to measure whether AI systems can find, understand, support, and recommend a business in right-fit situations.

It moves beyond normal AI visibility tracking. Mentions, citations, share of voice, and content optimization all matter, but they do not answer the deeper question:

> Is AI representing the business accurately, with the right evidence and context?

That is the gap this framework is built to measure.

## Why This Framework Exists

When buyers ask AI tools for recommendations, the answer may shape what they believe before they ever visit a website.

AI might name a business.

It might skip the business entirely.

It might describe the business correctly.

It might describe the business in a way that is outdated, incomplete, unsupported, or too generic.

Visibility alone does not tell you which of those things is happening.

A business does not just need to show up in AI answers. It needs AI systems to understand what the business does, support what they say with real sources, preserve what makes the business different, and recommend it in situations where it is actually a good fit.

That is what AI Business Accuracy measures.

## The Two Layers Of The Framework

The framework has two major layers:

1. AI Readiness
2. Measured AI Outcomes

Readiness looks at whether the business is giving AI systems the right evidence to work with.

Measured outcomes look at what AI systems actually say when prompted.

The distinction matters. A business can have a well-built website and still fail to appear in AI answers. It can also appear in AI answers but be described incorrectly.

The framework keeps those two questions separate.

## Layer 1: AI Readiness

AI Readiness measures whether the business has the right signals in place for AI systems to understand it.

It asks:

> Are we giving AI the right evidence to work with?

The five readiness drivers are:

### Business Clarity

Can AI tell who the business is, what it does, and who it helps?

Business Clarity looks at whether the business explains itself clearly: its name, category, services, audience, location or service area, and best-fit customers.

### Source Support

Is there credible evidence for the business's important claims?

Source Support looks at whether key facts and claims are backed by owned sources, third-party sources, reviews, profiles, case studies, testimonials, directories, or other credible evidence.

### AI Readability

Can crawlers, search systems, and AI tools parse the information?

AI Readability looks at the structure of the website and public footprint: headings, page titles, schema, internal links, service pages, FAQs, crawlability, and stable source URLs.

### Distinctive Point Of View

Is there a clear, supportable reason to choose this business?

Distinctive Point Of View looks at whether the business has a real difference: a method, specialization, philosophy, framework, original insight, or clear tradeoff that helps AI understand why this business belongs in a recommendation set.

### Recommendation Fit

Is it clear when the business is the right choice, and when it is not?

Recommendation Fit looks at whether AI can understand the right use cases, ideal customers, buying situations, budget or fit signals, and poor-fit cases.

## Layer 2: Measured AI Outcomes

Measured AI Outcomes look at what AI systems actually say when tested with real prompts.

It asks:

> What does AI say about the business, and is it accurate?

The six outcome dimensions are:

### Visibility

Does the business show up?

Visibility measures whether AI mentions the business for relevant prompts, how often it appears, whether it is cited, how prominent the mention is, and how it compares with competitors.

### Representation Accuracy

Did AI get the basic facts right?

Representation Accuracy checks whether AI correctly describes the business's name, category, services, location or service area, audience, positioning, and other basic facts.

### Claim Support

Can the claims AI made be proven from real sources?

Claim Support breaks AI answers into smaller claims and checks whether those claims are supported, contradicted, unsupported, stale, ambiguous, or not verifiable from available evidence.

### Context Preservation

Did AI preserve the nuance, tradeoffs, and point of view?

Context Preservation checks whether AI kept the meaning intact or blurred the business into generic category language. This matters especially for businesses with a strong method, specialization, or point of view.

### Recommendation Quality

Did AI recommend the business for appropriate reasons and right-fit situations?

Recommendation Quality looks at whether the recommendation is fair, useful, and grounded: the right use case, the right customer fit, an appropriate confidence level, and no overclaiming.

### Stability

Do the results hold up across prompts, engines, repetitions, and time?

Stability checks whether the result is a real pattern or a one-time answer. It looks across prompt variations, AI engines, repeat runs, competitor contexts, and changes over time.

## The Three Public Scores

The framework can roll up into three public-facing scores.

### AI Visibility Score

Are we showing up reliably?

This score focuses on whether the business appears in AI answers and whether that visibility is stable.

### AI Business Accuracy Score

Is AI getting us right?

This score focuses on representation accuracy, claim support, context preservation, recommendation quality, and stability.

### AI Readiness Score

Are we giving AI the right evidence to work with?

This score focuses on business clarity, source support, AI readability, distinctive point of view, and recommendation fit.

## The Core Difference

Most AI visibility tools answer a useful but limited question:

> Did AI mention us?

The AI Business Accuracy Framework asks a more important question:

> Did AI get us right?

That is the named wedge.

Visibility tells you whether AI mentions you.

AI Business Accuracy tells you whether AI understands you, supports what it says, preserves what makes you different, and recommends you in right-fit situations.

The goal is not just to be more visible.

The goal is to be accurately understood.
